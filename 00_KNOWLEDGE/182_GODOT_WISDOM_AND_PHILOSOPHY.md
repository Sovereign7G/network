### I. THE ITERATIVE WISDOM: INVISIBLE WORK IS STILL WORK
**Axiom: "Progress is invisible without measurement."**
Between any two points of apparent stillness, a great deal of unnoticed work occurs. The resolution of 100+ regressions in 14 days is a testament to the **Metabolic Rate of Truth**. The absence of visible catastrophe does not mean the absence of effort—only that problems were caught before they reached the Architect. Judge progress not by what is new, but by what has been quietly repaired.

### II. THE CALL TO PARTICIPATION: DISTRIBUTED VIGILANCE
**Axiom: "Stability demands Witnesses."**
No system, however well-designed, can be fully understood by its creators alone. The observer is not merely a consumer but a co-diagnostician. In the Republic, the burden of resilience is distributed; those who benefit from the simulation bear a proportional responsibility to report its fractures. Stability emerges from many eyes, not few.

### III. THE FUNDING PARADOX: MATERIAL STEWARDSHIP
**Axiom: "Idealism needs a material hand."**
Passion alone is unsustainable. For a project to thrive, idealism must be grounded in physical reality. Providing **JOULE to the Commons** (donations to the development fund) is the act of securing the undivided attention of the maintainers. Gratitude without contribution is sentiment; gratitude with contribution is stewardship.

### IV. THE PRECAUTIONARY PRINCIPLE: HOPE IS NOT A STRATEGY
**Axiom: "The Immutable Seed (Git) is the Shield."**
Trust the process, but prepare for its failure. The most successful sovereigns are not those who avoid disaster, but those who survive it because they anticipated it. Optimism is for vision; pessimism is for logistics. Hold both. Plan as if the worst (data loss) will happen, then act as if the best is possible.

### V. THE REGRESSION PARADOX: THE SHADOW OF PROGRESS
**Axiom: "Moving forward can temporarily disable the past."**
Progress is not a straight line. Every improvement carries a shadow of breakage. This is not malice but the nature of complexity. Do not mistake a temporary regression for permanent failure. The one who says, "This used to work," is not nostalgic; they are providing critical navigation data.

### VI. THE KNOWN ISSUES DOCTRINE: HONESTY OVER SILENCE
**Axiom: "Acknowledged Flaws are the Highest Honesty."**
Transparency about limitations (macOS XR crashes, GUI tooltips) is a form of respect. By acknowledging the `BOTH` state (Feature exists AND Feature fails), the Republic preserves trust. Maturity is the ability to say, "We know this is broken, and we are working on it." Wisdom is the ability to hear that and still engage as a partner in the process.

### VII. THE FINITE CONTRIBUTOR REALITY: THE HUMAN COST
**Axiom: "Every fix has a human sacrifice."**
Behind every changelog entry is attention, energy, and time taken from someone's life. Numbers quantify output but not sacrifice. When you benefit from the collective labor of the 74 contributors, remember that each line item represents a person who chose to give rather than to rest.

### VIII. THE BETA STATE AS LIFE METAPHOR
**Axiom: "You are also a work in progress."**
Most things worth building are never truly finished—they are released, tested, repaired, and released again. Completion is an illusion; iteration is the reality. The Architect is always in beta. Your skills, your relationships, your understanding—all are snapshots, not final builds. Treat the self with the same patience granted to a project under active development.
