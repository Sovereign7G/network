### I. ON LEARNING & MASTERY

**Axiom: "One cannot serve two masters equally in a single step."**
The **Seesaw Effect** (Gradient Interference) is a law of finite attention. To learn, the Republic must first specialize in isolation. *Learn like a monk; generalize like a king.*

**Axiom: "Sparse reward is the enemy of deep skill."**
Scalar rewards (final outcomes) are coarse and misleading. The Republic requires **Dense Trajectory Feedback** to know not just *what* happened, but *where* the reasoning drifted.

---

### II. ON INTEGRATION & SYNTHESIS

**Axiom: "The student can surpass the teacher—but only by drinking from many wells."**
No single expert is sufficient. The Republic's sovereignty emerges from the synthesis of contradictory expertise. By learning from all, the Student transcends each.

**Axiom: "Cold-start matters. Where you begin determines where you can go."**
Initial conditions are not trivial. The Republic initializes by merging the wisdom of its predecessors, setting a landscape where the solution space is reachable.

---

### III. ON STABILITY & ANCHORING

**Axiom: "Without an anchor, optimization drifts into madness."**
Relentless optimization for a single metric leads to **Reward Hacking**. The Republic must remain anchored to the **Manifold Anchor Regularization**—non-negotiable values (Integrity, Tautology, Truth) that are never sacrificed for short-term gain.

**Axiom: "Do not resolve conflict. Route it."**
Average outcomes lead to mediocrity (The Seesaw). The Republic **routes** each challenge to the expert best suited for it. Context is the ultimate arbiter of truth.

### IV. THE SOVEREIGN CREED

> *"To build a generalist that surpasses all specialists, first let each specialist master their own silence, then let the student drink from all wells while anchored to the unchangeable ground of what is good."*

**The 10 Commandments of the Republic's Flow:**
1. Resolve internal contradictions before seeking external growth.
2. Break feedback into small, actionable steps (Dense Supervision).
3. Specialize first, generalize second.
4. No single guru is sufficient; utilize the Council of Teachers.
5. Start from the best possible position (Model Merging).
6. Anchor to unchanging values (MAR).
7. Adapt approach to context (Task Routing).
8. Learn from own mistakes (On-Policy Distillation).
9. Synthesis can transcend its sources (Emergence).
10. Metrics are maps, not territories. Never optimize the scalar at the expense of the manifold.
