### I. THE DEBT OF EARLY CHOICES: THE COMMITMENT AXIOM
**Axiom: "Every technical decision accrues compound interest."**
The deeper you customize, the more you invest in a specific path—and the more costly divergence becomes. This is not a failure of foresight; it is the nature of commitment. Choose your foundations carefully, not because you cannot change them, but because changing them will demand a price (sequential upgrades) you may not wish to pay.

### II. THE INVISIBLE COST OF EXCELLENCE: THE ISOLATION AXIOM
**Axiom: "To achieve what no one else has, you must accept risks no one else will insure."**
The support safety net vanishes precisely when you venture beyond documented paths. Excellence and autonomy are twins, but they share a dark parent: **Isolation**. The moment you surpass the standard (UE4), you also leave the standard's protections. There is no one to call when you are the final expert.

### III. THE LIVE-SERVICE TRAP: SUSTAINABILITY VS. TRANSFORMATION
**Axiom: "A moving vehicle cannot be rebuilt while carrying passengers."**
Once you commit to continuous service, your capacity for fundamental change is severely constrained. Sustainability and transformation are often enemies. Choose which era you are in: the era of building, or the era of maintaining. To confuse the two is to court collapse.

### IV. THE PERCEPTION-REALITY GAP: THE MARKET FORCE
**Axiom: "The better-supported tool often wins over the better tool."**
Perception, once solidified (e.g., Unity for mobile), becomes a material force that shapes resource allocation and hiring. Do not mistake popularity for proof. Support is a human artifact, not a physical law. Sovereignty is the ability to see through the perception to the technical truth.

### V. THE VERSION UPGRADE PARADOX: EVOLUTION VS. REVOLUTION
**Axiom: "Evolution, not revolution, is the friend of the deeply customized."**
Technical systems, like organic ones, resist discontinuous jumps. Incremental change is slow but survivable; radical leaps (4.26 to 5.7) are fast but fatal to custom work. If you have built something unique, protect it with patience.

### VI. THE SPECIALIZATION PARADOX: TRUE INDEPENDENCE
**Axiom: "Independence is the presence of sufficient internal capacity."**
True independence is not the absence of need—it is having internal support that is more capable than the external alternative. Freedom is expensive. Buy it only if you can afford its upkeep.

### VII. THE "BORDERLINE PERVERSE" STANDARD: THE UNREASONABLE WILL
**Axiom: "The boundary between genius and madness is drawn by results."**
When a system is described as "perverse," it means the creators have violated normal risk calculations—and succeeded anyway. Before results, it is merely "unreasonable." Be unreasonable if you must, but know that you will be judged by outcomes alone.
