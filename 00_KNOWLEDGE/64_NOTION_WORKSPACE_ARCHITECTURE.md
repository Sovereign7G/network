## SUBSTRATE: BLOCK-BASED ARCHITECTURE | RELATIONAL DATABASES

### 1. THE ATOMIC UNIT: THE BLOCK
Notion’s fundamental substrate is the **Block**. Every piece of information—whether a paragraph, an image, a code snippet, or a database—is a discrete, polymorphic unit.
- **Modularity:** Blocks can be transformed, nested, and rearranged without loss of integrity.
- **Composition:** Complex knowledge structures are built through the hierarchical composition of atomic blocks.

### 2. THE RELATIONAL MANIFOLD: DATABASES
Notion replaces flat documents with multidimensional databases.
- **Properties:** Every page in a database is enriched with structured metadata (Tags, Dates, Relations).
- **Views:** The same data substrate can be rendered as a **Table**, **Kanban Board**, **Calendar**, **Timeline**, or **Gallery**, allowing for varied cognitive perspectives.
- **Relational Modeling:** Databases are linked via **Relations** (bidirectional connections) and **Rollups** (dynamic data aggregation), creating a self-evolving knowledge graph.

### 3. AUGMENTED COGNITION: NOTION AI
Integrated AI layers provide semantic search and generative synthesis across the entire block substrate.
- **Q&A:** Natural language retrieval across the workspace context.
- **Autofill:** Automated extraction of unstructured data into relational properties.
