# 121_MINT_PHILOSOPHY_RESIDENCY_OF_THE_LARGE

## I. PHILOSOPHICAL CORE
### 1. "Keep the heavy thing still. Move only the light thing that changes."
*A single trillion‑parameter model can sit idle while millions of minds swap in and out of it.*
**AXIOM**: In systems design, **immobility of the large** enables **agility of the many**. The base model is a landscape; adapters are temporary residents. You don't rebuild the continent for every house.

### 2. "Addressability is not residency."
*A catalog of one million policies does not require one million GPUs.*
**AXIOM**: A name is not a thing. A policy ID is not a loaded tensor. Separating *naming* from *materialization* is the difference between a library and a warehouse.

### 3. "Efficiency is not speed. Efficiency is what you don't move."
*18.3× step reduction comes from moving 1% of the data.*
**AXIOM**: The fastest operation is the one you avoid. Optimizing *what you move* is more powerful than optimizing *how fast you move it*.

## II. STRATEGIC WISDOM
### 4. Scale is not one number. Scale is three numbers.
> Scale Up (size) | Scale Down (efficiency) | Scale Out (count)
**AXIOM**: True infrastructure handles all three simultaneously—large models, cheap operations, and many policies.

### 5. Shared Infrastructure Economics
The base model is a *fixed cost* (amortized). Each adapter is a *marginal cost* (private). Good systems align technical architecture with economic incentives.

### 6. "Cold loading is normal, not failure."
Design for *cold catalogs* and *hot working sets*. If 99.9% of your data is not in memory at once, design a system where that is *expected*.
