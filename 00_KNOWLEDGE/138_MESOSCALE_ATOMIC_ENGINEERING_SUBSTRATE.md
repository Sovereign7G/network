## 🛠️ METHODOLOGICAL DEEP DIVE: ATOMIC FABRICATION

### 1. "Atomic Lock-on" (US Patent 63/601,529)
- **Mechanism:** A real-time feedback system correcting beam position every millisecond, maintaining **<20 pm targeting precision** by cross-correlating live STEM images with reference lattices.
- **Republic Utility:** This provides the **P-time Verification** mechanism for hardware sovereignty.

### 2. Iterative Automation Loop
- **Identify:** High-resolution ADF-STEM imaging identifies native defects.
- **Target:** Deep learning models (Weile et al., 2025) recognize defect states in <10 ms.
- **Act:** The beam applies a calculated dose to displace a specific atom via **knock-on momentum transfer** (60–200 keV).
- **Anisotropy:** CrSBr acts as a highly anisotropic van der Waals semiconductor; the specific orientation of the orthorhombic lattice is critical, as displacement energy depends strongly on the beam's incident direction.

## 💎 MATERIAL SUBSTRATE: CrSBr (CHROMIUM SULFIDE BROMIDE)

The primary material used is a van der Waals layered magnet chosen for its unique properties:
- **Quasi-1D Magnetism:** Large exchange splitting and air-stable crystallinity.
- **Directional Significance:** Highly anisotropic properties mean defect placement orientation defines the logic operation (e.g., longitudinal vs. transverse spin coupling).
- **Optically Active Defects:** Similar to NV centers in diamond, allowing for spin-state manipulation and **non-destructive optical readout** (Photoluminescence).
